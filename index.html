This is a personal webpage for myself, Nolan Kamitaki. I grew up in Hawaii before receiving a BA in statistics from Harvard University in 2015. I recently completed my PhD in Biomedical Informatics from Harvard Medical School under the supervision of Po-Ru Loh and Steve McCarroll in 2026. I am currently a postdoc in the McCarroll lab.

My interests are primarily in genetics, with a focus on characterizing the phenotypic consequences of alleles to learn new biology. Among alleles, I'm interested in structural variation, and among phenotypes, host-symbiont interactions. I also have an interest in designing experiments that maximize the power of downstream computational techniques. I realize these are a bit broad - see here for more detail
